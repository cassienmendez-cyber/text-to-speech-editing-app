# Glossary

Shared vocabulary for StoryScribe. These terms have specific meanings and are
used consistently across the product and its documentation.

- **Anchor** — The manuscript location a note attaches to. Anchors exist at four
  levels: sentence, paragraph, scene, and chapter. Notes can be re-anchored
  without being deleted.

- **Bookmark** — A saved location distinct from a revision note, used for
  favorite scenes, powerful moments, passages requiring reflection, or future
  expansion opportunities. Independently searchable.

- **Category** — A classification applied to a revision note (e.g. Pacing,
  Dialogue, Plot Hole). Includes built-in categories and author-created custom
  ones.

- **Drive Mode** — A constrained, hands-free experience for capturing revision
  ideas while driving. Eyes on the road, hands on the wheel.

- **Driving Confidence Setting** — How aggressively notes are confirmed while
  driving: BEGINNER (always confirm), STANDARD (confirm only on low
  transcription confidence), or EXPERT (auto-save).

- **Emergency Note** — A capture that preserves an unexpected breakthrough idea
  without interrupting playback.

- **Emotional Reaction Tag** — A quick reaction label (e.g. Exciting, Scary,
  Confusing) used to surface trends across the manuscript.

- **Quick Note** — A short idea captured immediately and stored in the Inbox.

- **Read Like a Reader Mode** — A mode that hides comments, analytics, and
  revision indicators so the author experiences the story as a reader would.

- **Revision Note** — A contextual note attached to the manuscript, storing a
  text transcription, the original voice recording, references, a timestamp, and
  surrounding context.

- **Revision Pass** — A named editing pass (e.g. Tension Pass, Dialogue Pass).
  Notes may belong to one or multiple passes.

- **Revision History** — The tracked record of accepted changes, including
  original and revised text and the ability to restore previous versions.

- **AI Mode** — The author-selected level of AI involvement: OFF, SUGGEST,
  ANALYZE, or COLLABORATE. AI never modifies manuscript text without explicit
  approval.

- **Wake Phrase** — A spoken trigger (e.g. "Hey StoryScribe") that activates
  voice capture, customizable by the author.
